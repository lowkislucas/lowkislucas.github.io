Why is Mental Health Important?


In the age of the internet, mental health is becoming an increasingly relevant issue. Mental health is all encompassing in our lives and is connected to our emotional, psychological and social well-being. Mental health affects how we think, feel and live our lives.


Traditional mental health resources do not account for the rise of the internet and mental illness among young people. They are often slow, old, expensive and do not address the real problems in the internet age.

Why are Gamers susceptible to bad mental health?

Escapism

Video games are commonly used by many people across the world as a coping method which they use in order to temporarily escape from reality. This is because, especially with the advancement of technology, game developers are easily able to create virtual worlds that can replicate reality and can provide a great temporary escape. So already heading in, many gamers are at a disadvantage in terms of positive thinking.

Toxicity

Now combine this with the rampant amount of toxicity and negative comments on the internet within the video games, this can and often lead to the mental health problem being heavily augmented. And although other competitive activities all have banter, the level of cyberbullying that occurs online is far higher than any other sport, as it is even uncommon to see hate speech being thrown around. This is due to the inability to regulate messages sent online.

Addiction

Another reason why mental health problems are highly susceptible to gamers, is because like with all forms of escapism, this can lead to an addiction. For many, especially ones who are competitive gamers, find the comfort within their games and because their previous time investment feels much better returning to the game. However this is often problematic because the toxic environment they spend so much time in pushes towards their declining mental health creating a negative cycle.
