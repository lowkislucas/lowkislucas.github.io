What can we do about it?

Take Breaks

The most important and best way to maintain good mental health is to take consistent breaks between gaming sessions. Often players experience deteriorating mental health (which often leads to bad performance in game) but continue to play the game which continuously makes the problem worse. To stop this, whenever you are feeling that the game has started to become a source of stress, take a step back and realize that taking a break can help. As for competitive games, taking a few day break is often beneficial towards resetting and starting from a fresh start. A misconception many players have is that they worry, taking a break will build rust and hinder their overall improvement, however this is often not the case as rust takes a lot longer than a few days to set in and on the optimistic side can pull players out of a rut.

/mute all

As for in game, the best tool to reduce the mental health strain is to use the option “mute all”. This setting is called different things across different games however all games have an option to block incoming messages. Toggling this option can block out the negativity that is extremely prevalent within the video game. And although many would point to the fact that muting is a competitive disadvantage, the benefits far outweigh the negatives being able to play consistently and sustainably leads to overall smoother progression in skill than being burnt out from mental health problems.
